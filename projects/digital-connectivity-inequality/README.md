# Digital Connectivity and Income Inequality

## Research Question
To what extent is digital connectivity associated with within-country changes in income inequality?

## Motivation
Digitalization is transforming economies worldwide, but its distributional effects remain unclear. 
This project analyzes whether increased internet access leads to more inclusive economic outcomes.

## Data
- World Bank (WDI)
- International Telecommunication Union (ITU)
- Panel data: 2005–2024
- 150+ countries

## Methodology
- Panel data econometrics
- Two-way fixed effects model
- Controls:
  - Unemployment
  - Vulnerable employment
  - GDP growth
  - Inflation

## Key Findings
- Digital connectivity is negatively associated with income inequality
- Effects are stronger in developing countries
- Labor market conditions remain key drivers of inequality
